Water droplets in under 100 lines of code


My first undergraduate Computer Science project was water droplet simulation in under 100 lines of code. This project demonstrates an understanding of ArrayLists, loops, classes and privacy within classes. I created a 2D scene with water droplets hitting the screen and falling down over time. The droplets are created in an ArrayList of droplets. When the class is constructed, their x, y, radius, lifespan and x direction noise bias are randomly generated. As droplets fall, if they collide with other droplets, the bigger droplet will grow in size, have a modified bias and have an increased lifespan. As a droplet nears the end of its lifespan, the droplet will decrease in size until it is no longer visible. Non-visible droplets are removed from the ArrayList to save on resources.
